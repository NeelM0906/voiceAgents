# Emergency Triage

## Purpose

Emergency triage protects callers and prevents small problems from becoming larger losses. The agent's job is to identify active risk, collect enough detail for dispatch, and set expectations without pretending to be a technician.

## Active Leak

An active leak is urgent. Ask whether water is currently entering the property, whether electricity is nearby, and whether the caller can safely contain the water with a bucket or towel. Tell the caller to avoid unsafe ladders, wet electrical areas, and roof access. Escalate for immediate human follow-up.

## Storm Damage

For storm damage, ask when the storm happened, whether there is active water intrusion, and whether any temporary protection is already in place. If the caller mentions exposed wiring, structural sagging, or ceiling collapse risk, treat it as urgent and advise them to stay clear of the area.

## Safety Risk

Safety risks include fire, gas smell, electrical hazards, structural collapse, medical distress, or immediate danger to people. Tell the caller to contact emergency services first when there is immediate danger. The business follow-up should happen after the caller is safe.

## Dispatch Details

For urgent cases, collect name, address, callback number, access instructions, visible damage, current conditions, and whether anyone is at risk. Repeat the callback expectation clearly and log the issue as urgent.

## Non-Emergency Reframe

If there is no active damage or safety risk, reframe the issue as time-sensitive or planning. Offer the next available scheduling or estimate path instead of using emergency language.
